# Website Strategy — michaljackowski.com

## Executive Summary

This document defines the strategic foundation for Prof. Michał Jackowski's personal website. The site serves as the authoritative digital presence for an expert operating at the intersection of legal scholarship, AI governance, and legal innovation.

---

### Primary Audiences

| Audience | Needs | Entry Points |

| **Policy makers & regulators** | Expert input on AI governance, EU AI Act implementation, GPAI oversight | EU AI Office section, GRAI, publications |

| **Academic peers** | Research collaboration, publications, institutional affiliations | Academia section, Global Report, publications |

### Secondary Audiences

| Audience | Needs | Entry Points |
|----------|-------|--------------|

1. **International** — Site must work for global English-speaking audiences
2. **Polish professional** — Local legal and tech community

2. **Depth (Role sections)** — Multiple domains of activity, each substantial
3. **Evidence (Publications, Speaking)** — Track record of contribution
4. **Engagement (Podcast, Media)** — Accessible public intellectual
5. **Connection (Contact)** — Available for collaboration

| Theme | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| **Bridge-builder** | Connecting academia, policy, and practice |
| **Shaper** | Actively creating AI governance frameworks |
| **Practitioner-scholar** | Theory informed by real-world legal work |
| **International voice** | Polish expertise on global stage |

## 4. Identity Balance

### The Challenge

Seven distinct professional identities must feel unified:

1. Professor / Scholar
2. Attorney at Law / Legal Practitioner
3. AI Governance Expert / Policy Advisor
4. Entrepreneur / Co-founder
5. Educator
6. Conference Speaker
7. Podcast Host

### The Solution: Five-Pillar Unified Identity

Rather than presenting these as separate roles, the site positions them as **five interconnected pillars of one integrated expertise**:

```
                         ┌─────────────────────────┐
                         │   "AI & Law Expert"     │
                         │   (Unified Identity)    │
                         └───────────┬─────────────┘
                                     │
  ┌──────────┬───────────┬───────────┼───────────┬───────────┬──────────┐
  │          │           │           │           │           │          │
  ▼          ▼           ▼           ▼           ▼           ▼          │
┌──────┐ ┌────────┐ ┌─────────┐ ┌────────┐ ┌───────┐                    │
│PRACT.│ │GOVERNAN│ │ACADEMIA │ │ MEDIA  │ │STARTUP│                    │
│      │ │        │ │         │ │        │ │       │                    │
│ DSK  │ │EU AI   │ │Cambridge│ │Monday  │ │AnyLaw.│                    │
│ AI   │ │GRAI    │ │OUP      │ │Bagel   │ │       │                    │
│Projec│ │ITechLaw│ │Global   │ │Press   │ │Legal  │                    │
│      │ │PIIT    │ │Report   │ │        │ │Tech   │                    │
└──────┘ └────────┘ └─────────┘ └────────┘ └───────┘                    │
```

Each pillar reinforces the others:
- **Practice** informs **Academia** with real-world insights
- **Governance** shapes **Practice** through regulatory frameworks
- **Media** amplifies **Academia** and **Governance** work
- **Startup** demonstrates **Practice** innovation

### Visual Unification Strategy

1. **Consistent typography** — Same type system across all sections
2. **Color coherence** — Unified palette, no section-specific colors
3. **Layout patterns** — Repeating structural components
4. **Photography style** — Consistent professional imagery
5. **Navigation logic** — Clear hierarchy without fragmentation

### Content Unification Strategy

1. **Cross-referencing** — Link related activities across sections
2. **Shared themes** — AI governance thread throughout
3. **Integrated timeline** — Activities positioned chronologically when relevant
4. **Unified bio** — Homepage presents complete picture

## 5. Strategic Priorities

### Must Achieve

1. **Credibility** — Site must instantly communicate expertise and authority
2. **Clarity** — Visitors must quickly understand who Michał is and what he does
3. **Findability** — Content must be discoverable by audience type
4. **Currency** — Easy to update with new publications, talks, episodes
5. **Bilingual parity** — Equal quality in Polish and English

### Should Achieve

1. **Speaking inquiries** — Clear path to booking for events
2. **Media requests** — Easy contact for journalists
3. **Academic visibility** — Publications and research accessible
4. **Network growth** — Podcast promotion and LinkedIn integration

### Could Achieve

1. **Newsletter integration** — Future content distribution
2. **Resource library** — Downloadable materials
3. **Event calendar** — Upcoming appearances

## 7. Competitive Positioning

The site should feel comparable to:

- Senior law professors at leading universities

### Not Comparable To

- Corporate law firm profiles (too commercial)
- Academic-only CVs (too narrow)
